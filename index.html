Rucksack Magazine is a printed bi-annual photography and travel publication dedicated to storytelling. We began in 2016 as an online journal and have recently printed our eighth volume of the magazine called Coast. We have also recently published our first hardback book called Elements, made in partnership with Lannoo Publishers. Our magazines, books and zines feature photo essays, travel stories and fictional pieces exploring a particular theme for each issue. We work as a platform for new and emerging photographers and writers, connecting a global community of creative storytellers.

Volume Eight: Coast (Gold Edition)

The stories in this issue explore the theme of coast; vast, open spaces in a constant state of change.

Through ‘Coast’, we showcase how these landscapes are more than simply where land meets water. They serve as pathways to places and feelings etched in memory, a connection with the emotion of our past. We journey through the golden expanse of the Californian coastline, visit remote Scottish coves and drive along dramatic Portuguese cliff drops. Through exploring the Oregon coastline and South West Coast Path, we discover a sense of freedom that emerges when near the water. There is a lightness to our pages, a celebration of the unfiltered happiness only a place such as the coastline can bring.

  • This publication is 176 pages, perfect bound and printed on uncoated, recycled paper. The cover is embossed with a hessian texture.

  • Featuring photography by Spencer Backman, Jared Chambers, Brian Chorski, Giulia Gartner, Alice Greenfield, Lydia Harper, Conni Koscina, Mirko Nicholson and J.K Winders.

  • Featuring writing by Spencer Backman, Mark Casagranda, Will Glover, Laura Pendlebury and Jennifer Smith.

  • In response to new EU VAT regulations, we are currently unable to ship to the EU. Please use Coffee Table Mags or any other EU stockist.

  • Ships worldwide: delivery times varying based on location. Please allow up to 3 business days for processing prior to shipping. For more information on shipping please visit here.

  • Printed and published in London, September 2021.
